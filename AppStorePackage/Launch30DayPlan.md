# IDPhoto Pro 30-Day Launch Plan

Use `Day 0` as the date your app is approved and released on the App Store.

| Day | Focus | Task | Output / KPI |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Day 0 | Launch readiness | Verify App Store page, screenshots, pricing, IAP, privacy/support links, and one-time purchase copy in the live listing. | Listing is live and accurate in the U.S. storefront. |
| Day 0 | Real-device QA | Run the full flow on a physical iPhone: choose template, import/take photo, edit, export, purchase, restore purchase. | No blocker in the primary user path. |
| Day 1 | Market setup | Record baseline metrics in App Analytics: impressions, product page views, conversion rate, first-time downloads, proceeds. | Baseline metrics sheet created. |
| Day 1 | Product pages | Create 3 Custom Product Pages for `US Passport`, `Schengen Visa`, and `Canada Visa / PR`. | 3 CPP pages ready with unique URLs. |
| Day 2 | Conversion test | Start 1 Product Page Optimization test. Only test screenshot order. | A/B test running. |
| Day 2 | Attribution prep | Use a tracking sheet to map each external post to the linked Custom Product Page. | Link tracking sheet ready. |
| Day 3 | Social launch | Post the first English launch posts on Reddit, X, and Facebook using the `US passport photo at home` angle. | 3 live posts published. |
| Day 3 | Landing page | Publish or update a simple support page or landing page that highlights offline privacy, one-time purchase, official sizes, and 4x6 print layout. | One canonical landing page link ready. |
| Day 4 | Search intent content | Publish 2 short English posts focused on `2x2 passport photo` and `4x6 print at Walgreens/CVS`. | 2 more live posts. |
| Day 4 | Review funnel | Verify that the in-app rating prompt only appears after successful usage and never conflicts with purchase prompts. | Rating flow validated. |
| Day 5 | Store audit | Review the first 5 screenshots again and confirm the first 3 clearly show: official size, offline privacy, 4x6 print, one-time purchase. | Screenshot priority locked. |
| Day 5 | Keywords | Review title, subtitle, keywords, and screenshot captions for U.S. search intent. | Metadata revision list created. |
| Day 6 | Data review | Check App Analytics and note early store conversion and download numbers. | First data checkpoint logged. |
| Day 7 | Week 1 review | Decide whether the default page or one of the CPP variants is getting better product page conversion. | Primary traffic page chosen. |
| Day 8 | U.S. focus | Publish a post or thread focused only on `How to print U.S. passport photos on 4x6 paper`. | 1 targeted post published. |
| Day 9 | Trust proof | Publish a post focused only on privacy: `All processing happens on-device. No upload.` | 1 privacy-focused post published. |
| Day 10 | Feedback mining | Collect every early user complaint from reviews, email, Reddit, and social replies. Group into `confusing`, `broken`, `trust issue`. | Feedback backlog created. |
| Day 11 | Listing revision | If product page conversion is under 15%, update the screenshot order or copy based on the current test results. | Revised listing prepared. |
| Day 12 | Paid conversion review | Check how many downloads reached the paywall and how many unlocked Pro. | Early purchase funnel measured. |
| Day 13 | Content expansion | Publish 2 posts targeting `Schengen visa photo size` and `Canada visa photo`. | 2 region-specific posts published. |
| Day 14 | Week 2 review | Compare `impressions -> page views -> downloads -> purchases`. Identify the weakest step. | Funnel diagnosis complete. |
| Day 15 | Fix priority | Choose the top 3 product issues that block trust or purchase: compliance score trust, export clarity, print layout clarity, or auto-fix quality. | Version update scope defined. |
| Day 16 | Ratings push | Encourage more ratings only from satisfied users: export success, multiple successful sessions, no recent prompt shown. | Smart rating trigger verified. |
| Day 17 | Community follow-up | Reply to every meaningful Reddit/X/Facebook comment with direct answers and product link only when relevant. | Social follow-up complete. |
| Day 18 | UGC assets | Prepare 3 new App Store or social visuals using the strongest benefit statements from early user feedback. | 3 new visual concepts ready. |
| Day 19 | Conversion test 2 | If needed, start a second Product Page Optimization test for screenshot headline wording. | Second test prepared or running. |
| Day 20 | Price check | Evaluate whether `US$4.99` is hurting conversion. Do not change price unless download-to-purchase data is already stable. | Pricing decision documented. |
| Day 21 | Week 3 review | Review proceeds, purchases, review count, average rating, and best-performing traffic source. | Week 3 summary written. |
| Day 22 | Update prep | Finalize the first post-launch version update focused on trust and export quality, not feature sprawl. | Update changelog drafted. |
| Day 23 | SEO-style content | Post 1 long-form answer or thread for `passport photo app without subscription` or `offline passport photo maker`. | 1 intent-rich post published. |
| Day 24 | Regional expansion | If U.S. traffic is stable, start pushing one Europe-focused page or post. If not, keep full focus on U.S. use cases. | Geographic focus decision made. |
| Day 25 | Trust assets | Add or refresh support FAQ content around privacy, acceptance expectations, 4x6 printing, and one-time purchase. | FAQ updated. |
| Day 26 | Version release | Submit the first post-launch update if the fixes are ready and materially improve trust or conversion. | New build submitted or scheduled. |
| Day 27 | Review request timing | Check whether review prompts and export success prompts still feel respectful after recent use sessions. | Prompt timing audited. |
| Day 28 | Funnel checkpoint | Recalculate: product page conversion, download-to-purchase conversion, average proceeds per day, and rating trend. | Near-30-day numbers logged. |
| Day 29 | Decision point | Decide whether to double down on U.S. passport, expand into visa niches, or revisit onboarding/paywall first. | Next 30-day strategy chosen. |
| Day 30 | Full retrospective | Write a concise launch review: what worked, what failed, what to ship next, and whether the app is on track toward `$1000/month`. | 30-day retrospective complete. |

## Target Benchmarks

| Metric | Good early target |
| --- | --- |
| Product page conversion | `15%+` |
| Download to paid unlock | `4%+` |
| Early average rating | `4.4+` |
| Refund / complaint pressure | Low and not concentrated on compliance trust |

## Weekly Review Questions

1. Are users understanding the difference between digital export and 4x6 print layout?
2. Is the app store page attracting the right users, or just broad low-intent traffic?
3. Does the compliance score feel trustworthy compared with the exported result?
4. Is the one-time purchase message clear enough before the user hits export?
5. Are most complaints about trust, usability, or country/spec coverage?
